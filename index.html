About

I am a PhD student in the Agricultural and Biological Engineering Department at the University of Florida. My research focuses on blockchain enabled data sharing and verification traceability in agriculture, with applications in carbon markets, smart farming, and cross sector data infrastructure.

Research

Verification traceability for agricultural data

Blockchain based infrastructure for data origin, transformation integrity, and system interoperability

I develop a platform that allows agricultural datasets to preserve verifiable evidence of origin, transformation history, and usage conditions as they move across heterogeneous environments.

Blockchain Agricultural data Traceability Data sharing

Projects

Agricultural data sharing platform

Open source implementation of DV, TI, and SI modules

Ongoing project implementing verification traceability functions as reusable components for agricultural data platforms and research workflows.

Fresh food supply chain and food loss accounting

Integration of tracking events, waste accounting, and Scope 3 GHG reporting

Conceptual framework that connects product provenance, food loss and waste measurements, and greenhouse gas accounting in retail and upstream supply chains.

Teaching and Mentoring

I mentor undergraduate students in data driven agriculture and support graduate level courses in process design and research proposal writing.
